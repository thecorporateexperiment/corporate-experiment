A Social Experiment

Would your company donate to a stranger, for no reason at all?

No product. No sponsorship. No charity. Just one honest question about corporate generosity — and a running tally of the answer.

The premise

I'm emailing publicly listed companies with a genuinely pointless request: donate a small, arbitrary amount to a complete stranger who is offering absolutely nothing in return.

It's part curiosity, part quiet satire. We assume large companies are generous when there's PR to gain or a tax line to fill — so what happens when there's neither? When the only thing on offer is the warm glow of pure, unprompted goodwill?

This page exists so anyone who receives the email — or stumbles across the project — can see it's exactly what it claims to be. Nothing hidden, nothing owed.

Want to take part?

Contribute any amount you like — even $100, if $10,000 is a stretch this quarter. Every contribution becomes a data point.

Make a contribution

Prefer to stay anonymous, or want your company named in the results? Just reply to the email and I'll honour whichever you choose.

Reasonable questions

Is this a scam?
No — and I'd be suspicious too. There's no invoice, no urgency, no pretence that you owe anything. It's a voluntary experiment, and this page is here precisely so you can verify that before deciding.
Are you a registered charity?
No, and I'm not claiming to be. Contributions aren't tax-deductible and aren't going to a cause. That's the whole point of the experiment.
What do you do with the money?
It's tallied as a result. The experiment measures whether generosity happens at all when nothing is offered in return — the amounts are the finding.
Will you keep emailing me?
No. One message per company, no follow-ups. The only reply you'll ever get from me is a thank-you, and only if you chose to contribute.
Can I see the results?
Yes. Aggregated findings will be published here once there's enough data to be interesting.
